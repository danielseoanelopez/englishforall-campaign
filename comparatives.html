① GRAMMAR · comparatives
📘 COMPARATIVES · comparar A con B

Para comparar dos cosas usamos el comparativo + than. Repasa y luego escucha al equipo comparar laptops, oficinas y personas.

+er · adj. cortos cheap → cheaper
fast → faster
big → bigger (doble g) · easy → easier
more · adj. largos more expensive
more comfortable
more reliable
★ Irregulares good → better
bad → worse
far → further
⚠ Watch out siempre than: A is faster than B
more bigger · more better
bigger · better

Adjetivos útiles para describir cosas y personas:

🎤Speaking: Compare two phones or laptops you know. Use cheaper, faster and better with than.

EMPIEZA ASÍThe black laptop is faster than the silver one, but it's more…

Marcos: Sofia, I need a new laptop for work. Can you help me choose?

Sofia: Sure! Look at these two. The silver one is cheaper, but the black one is faster.

Marcos: How big is the difference?

Sofia: The black one is more expensive, about two hundred dollars more. But it has a better battery.

Marcos: Is it heavier?

Sofia: A little. The silver one is lighter, so it's better for travel. But the screen is smaller.

Marcos: I think the black one is more powerful. The battery is more important for me.

Sofia: Good choice. It's also more reliable. I have the same one.

④ WRITING · en clase o tarea (HW)
✍️ COMPARE TWO THINGS

Compara dos cosas (dos teléfonos, dos ciudades, dos trabajos…) en 5–6 frases:

  • Usa 2 comparativos cortos (cheaper, faster, bigger…) + than
  • Usa 2 comparativos largos (more expensive, more comfortable…)
  • Usa 1 irregular (better / worse)
  • Termina con tu opinión: I think … is better because …
EXAMPLEMy new laptop is better than my old one. It is faster and lighter, and the screen is bigger. The old one was cheaper, but the battery was worse. My new laptop is more expensive, but it is more reliable for work. I think the new one is better because I travel a lot and it is more comfortable.
