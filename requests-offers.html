① GRAMMAR · requests & offers
📘 PEDIR & OFRECER · can / could / would

En el trabajo pedimos y ofrecemos cosas todo el día. Usamos modales + verbo base. Repasa y luego escucha las llamadas y chats del equipo.

↗ Requests (pedir) Can you help me?
Could you send it? (más cortés)
Can I have your number?
✋ Offers (ofrecer) I'll call the client.
Shall I help you?
Would you like a coffee?
💬 Responding Sure! / Of course.
No problem.
Sorry, I can't right now.
⚠ Watch out verbo base: Can you to send?
Can you send?
could > can (más formal)

Escalera de cortesía — de directo a muy cortés:

Can you…?› Could you…?› Would you mind…?
take a messagetomar un mensaje
call backdevolver la llamada
hold onesperar (en línea)
speaking(al teléfono) "con él/ella habla"
a favorun favor
send / forwardenviar / reenviar
attach a fileadjuntar un archivo
replyresponder
share my screencompartir pantalla
book a roomreservar una sala
confirmconfirmar
would you mind…?¿te molestaría…? (muy cortés)

③ LISTENING · escucha y comprende
🎧 AUDIO 1 · A CALL FROM A CLIENT

Marcos atiende una llamada. La clienta quiere hablar con Sofia. Fíjate en los pedidos (could you…?) y ofrecimientos (I'll…).

⏱ 1:01🗣 Marcos 🇲🇽 + Emma 🇺🇸

01Who does Emma want to speak to?

02Why can't she speak to her now?

03What does Marcos offer to do?

🎤Speaking: You answer the phone and the caller wants your boss, who is busy. What do you say? Use Can I…? and I'll….

EMPIEZA ASÍI'm sorry, she's in a meeting. Can I take a message? I'll ask her to…

Marcos: Good morning, English for All, this is Marcos speaking. How can I help you?

Emma: Hi, this is Emma from Bright Company. Could I speak to Sofia, please?

Marcos: I'm sorry, Sofia is in a meeting right now. Can I take a message?

Emma: Yes, please. Could you ask her to call me back this afternoon?

Marcos: Of course. Can I have your number?

Emma: Sure, it's five five five, zero one four two. And could you tell her it's about the new project?

Marcos: No problem, Emma. I'll give her the message. Would you like her to email you too?

Emma: Yes, please. That would be great. Thank you so much!

Marcos: You're welcome. Have a nice day. Goodbye!

🎧 AUDIO 2 · A CHAT AT WORK

Sofia le pide ayuda a Marcos con un informe. Fíjate en lo que pide y lo que Marcos ofrece.

⏱ 0:47🗣 Sofia 🇬🇧 + Marcos 🇲🇽

01What does Sofia ask Marcos to check?

02What does Marcos offer to do as well?

03When does Sofia want it sent to the client?

🎤Speaking: A colleague is busy. Offer to help with two things. Use I'll… and Shall I…?

EMPIEZA ASÍDon't worry, I'll check the numbers now. Shall I also…?

Sofia: Marcos, can you help me with the report? I'm very busy today.

Marcos: Sure, Sofia. What do you need?

Sofia: Could you check the numbers on page two? I think there's a mistake.

Marcos: No problem. I'll check them now. Do you want me to fix the formatting too?

Sofia: Yes, please, that would be great. And could you send it to the client before five?

Marcos: Of course. Shall I add the new logo as well?

Sofia: Good idea! Thank you so much, Marcos. You're a star.

Marcos: Happy to help. I'll send it to you first to review.

🎧 AUDIO 3 · A NEW INTERN NEEDS HELP

Lucía es nueva y necesita ayuda. Marcos ofrece ayuda. Fíjate en "Would you like me to…?" y "Shall I…?".

⏱ 0:54🗣 Marcos 🇲🇽 + Lucía 🇺🇸

01What is Lucía's problem?

02What does Marcos offer to do first?

03Can Lucía message Marcos with more questions?

🎤Speaking: A new colleague is lost. Offer two kinds of help. Use Would you like me to…? and Shall I…?

EMPIEZA ASÍWould you like me to share my screen? And shall I send you the…?

Lucía: Marcos, can I ask you a question? I'm new here and I'm a bit lost.

Marcos: Of course, Lucía! How can I help?

Lucía: Could you show me how to log into the system?

Marcos: Sure, no problem. Would you like me to share my screen?

Lucía: Yes, please. That would help a lot.

Marcos: Okay. And shall I send you the team guide too? It explains everything.

Lucía: That would be great, thank you. Can I message you if I have more questions?

Marcos: Of course! Send me a message any time. I'm always happy to help.

Lucía: Thank you so much, Marcos. You're very kind.

🎧 AUDIO 4 · A VOICEMAIL OF FAVORS

Sofia le deja un mensaje a Marcos con varios pedidos para mañana. Fíjate en lo que pide y lo que ella ofrece.

⏱ 0:32🗣 Sofia 🇬🇧

01What does Sofia ask Marcos to prepare?

02What does Sofia offer to bring?

03What time does Sofia suggest they meet to check everything?

🎤Speaking: Leave a short voicemail asking a colleague for two favors and offering one thing. Use Could you…? and I'll…

EMPIEZA ASÍHi, it's me. Could you prepare the slides? And could you also…? I'll bring…

Sofia: Hi Marcos, it's Sofia. I have a few favors to ask before tomorrow's meeting. First, could you prepare the slides? Second, can you book a meeting room for ten o'clock? Also, would you mind calling the client to confirm? Oh, and I'll bring the coffee, so don't worry about that. If you can't do everything, just let me know and I'll help. Shall we meet at nine to check everything? Thanks a lot, Marcos. See you tomorrow!

④ WRITING · en clase o tarea (HW)
✍️ A WORK CHAT

Escribe un chat de trabajo corto (5–6 líneas). Pide ayuda con 2 cosas y ofrece 1 cosa:

  • Pide con Can you…? y Could you…? (verbo base)
  • Ofrece con I'll… o Shall I…?
  • Responde a un pedido (Sure! / Of course / Sorry, I can't…)
  • Usa una palabra de chat/teléfono (send, attach, call back, reply)
EXAMPLEHi Ana! Could you send me the report before lunch? And can you attach the photos too? — Sure, no problem! I'll send everything now. Shall I also forward it to the client? — Yes, please, that would be great. Thank you! — Of course, happy to help.
