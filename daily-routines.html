① GRAMMAR · present simple
📘 PRESENT SIMPLE & FREQUENCY

Usamos el present simple para rutinas y cosas que pasan siempre. Repasa rápido y luego escucha la historia.

✓ Affirmative I / you / we / they work
he / she / it works (+s)
✗ Negative I don't work
she doesn't work
? Questions Do you work?
Does she work?
What time do you start?
⚠ Watch out She works here.
She work here.
He doesn't work. (no "doesn't works")

Adverbios de frecuencia — van antes del verbo principal: I always check emails. · con to be van después: She is never late.

🎤Speaking: Describe Sofia's morning routine. Use always and usually.

EMPIEZA ASÍShe usually starts at nine o'clock, and she always checks…

Sofia: Hi Marcos, welcome to the team! How are you today?

Marcos: Hi Sofia, thank you! I'm great, but a little nervous. It's my first day.

Sofia: Don't worry. Let me tell you about our day. We work remotely, so we have a simple routine.

Marcos: Perfect. What time does the team start?

Sofia: We usually start at nine o'clock. I always check my emails first, and then we have a short meeting.

Marcos: A meeting every day?

Sofia: Yes, every morning. We call it the standup. It's quick. We only talk for ten minutes.

Marcos: I like that. And when do you take a break?

Sofia: I usually take a break at eleven, and we have lunch at one. In the afternoon, I often write reports.

Marcos: What time do you finish?

Sofia: I normally finish at five, but I never work on weekends. Family time!

Marcos: That sounds great. Thank you, Sofia.

🎧 AUDIO 2 · MARCOS'S DAY

⏱ 0:49🗣 Marcos 🇲🇽

01What does Marcos always need in the morning?

03How often does Marcos work late?

🎤Speaking: What is Marcos's morning routine? Tell it in 2–3 sentences.

EMPIEZA ASÍHe usually wakes up at seven, has breakfast and a coffee, and then…

Marcos: Hi everyone, I'm Marcos, the new developer. Let me tell you about my day. I live in Mexico, so I'm in a different time zone. I usually wake up at seven o'clock. First, I have breakfast and drink a big coffee. I always need coffee! Then I log on at eight thirty. I check my messages and start my tasks. I often work on the website in the morning, because it's quiet. I usually have lunch at one, and sometimes I go for a short walk. In the afternoon, I have meetings with the team. I rarely work late, but on busy days I finish at six. I never forget to log off and say goodbye. That's my day!

🎧 AUDIO 3 · FINDING A MEETING TIME

Sofia y Marcos buscan una buena hora para la reunión diaria entre zonas horarias.

⏱ 0:59🗣 Sofia 🇬🇧 + Marcos 🇲🇽

01Why is nine o'clock (Sofia's time) a bad time for Marcos?

02What time do they agree to meet (Sofia's time)?

03Does Marcos work on Fridays?

🎤Speaking: When does the team meet, and why is that time good for Marcos?

EMPIEZA ASÍThey meet every day at two o'clock (Sofia's time), which is…

Sofia: Marcos, we need a good time for our daily standup. What time do you start work?

Marcos: I usually start at eight thirty, my time. But you're five hours ahead, right?

Sofia: Yes, exactly. When I start at nine, it's only four in the morning for you!

Marcos: Oh, that's too early. Do you ever have meetings in the afternoon?

Sofia: Sometimes, but I prefer the morning. How about two o'clock, my time?

Marcos: Two o'clock for you is nine for me. That's perfect. I'm always free at nine.

Sofia: Great. So we meet every day at two, my time. Do you work on Fridays?

Marcos: Yes, I do, but I usually finish early on Fridays.

Sofia: No problem. Our meeting is short. Welcome again, Marcos!
